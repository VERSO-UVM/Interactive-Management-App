Interpretive Structural Method Explained

Interpretive Structural Modeling (ISM) is a systematic methodology used to analyze and understand complex relationships within a given problem. It involves a structured process where participants identify key factors affecting the issue at hand. These factors are then explored to determine how they interact and influence one another. ISM then builds a visual representation, effectively identifying the most critical factors and presenting the results in a clearly articulated model. By prioritizing actions based on impact, ISM facilitates a clear path towards resolving complex problems.
